Determining the position of a train is a very interesting subject. Our MTi-G sensor could be helpful for you. However, please note the MTi-G is an orientation sensor in the first place and due to its integrated GPS receiver it can also give you a position and velocity. These last two values are then also enhanced by the use of the MEMS accelerometers of the IMU part.

 

Still the MTi can give you a relatively accurate position estimate when the GPS fix is lost (in e.g. a tunnel) but the accuracy will degrade with time. Because we use MEMS accelerometers, this degradation will be relatively fast, see below:

 

The MTi-G: You have access to the sensor data and thus you can use this data for every purpose you may have for it. Please note that the MTi-G uses MEMS sensors, which are very accurate for their low price. The strength of the MTi-G is that, due to the sensor fusion algorithm, the orientation can be estimated accurately and without any drift.

 

The orientation is estimated with an accuracy of about 0.5 degree (according to the specifications), which means that after substracting the gravity from the accellerometer readings, you will end up with an accelleration residue of about 0.1 m/s2 on the other axes. Integrating this residue will result in drift of the velocity or position because there is no reference. Also e.g. the noise (which is relatively large for MEMS) will cause integration drifts (the latter is much smaller than the other effect described).

 

Example of calculating the velocity or position based on a residue of 0.1 m/s2:

 

Speed:

integrating a 0.1 m/s2 once:

after one second: max error 0.1 m/s

after five seconds: max error 0.5 m/s

 

Position:

integrating a 0.1 m/s2 twice:

after one second: max error 0.05 m.

after five seconds: max error 1.25 m.

 

With the values above, you need to take the accuracy of the GPS receiver into account (see leaflet attached).

 

About the features you request: I think the MTi-G with its development kit does meet about these requirements. Only we do not output the data in NMEA format, but in our own digital format on a RS232 interface.

 

Please find attached also the manual for the MTi-G in which you may find more useful information.
